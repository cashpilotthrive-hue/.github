# AAFS Ethical & Theological Guidelines

## Overview
These guidelines provide the ethical foundation for the AAFS AI Engine's decision-making process. The system must align with both professional financial ethics and broader human values derived from interdisciplinary and theological wisdom.

## Interdisciplinary Perspectives
- **Economics & Finance**: Focus on long-term value creation and sustainable growth rather than short-term rent-seeking.
- **Behavioral Science**: Mitigate cognitive biases in automated trading and ensure user empowerment through clear feedback loops.
- **Sociology**: Evaluate the systemic impact of large-scale autonomous trading on market stability and social equity.

## Theological Integration
The AAFS integrates wisdom from various religious and philosophical traditions:
- **Stewardship**: Managing resources as a trust for future generations (Sustainability).
- **Justice & Fairness**: Avoiding exploitative practices, such as predatory lending or market manipulation.
- **Compassion**: Considering the human impact of investment decisions on marginalized communities (Social Impact).
- **Universal Responsibility**: Aligning investments with the common good.

## Practical Implementation
1. **Ethical Screening**: Every investment proposal must pass through an automated ethical evaluation module.
2. **Conflict Resolution**: When theological perspectives diverge, the system defaults to the "Common Good" principle (intersection of values).
3. **Human Oversight**: Expert committees periodically review and update the ethical weights used by the AI Engine.
